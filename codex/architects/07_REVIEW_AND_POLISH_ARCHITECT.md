# 07_REVIEW_AND_POLISH_ARCHITECT.md - Final Review and Premium Polish

## Purpose

Use this architect for final review passes, executive polish, duplicate-proof checks, and deciding whether a change actually improves the site.

This is the quality gate for Round 3.

---

## Review Modes

Run only the review modes relevant to the task.

### Recruiter Scan

Ask:

- Can a recruiter understand who Bryan is within 5 seconds?
- Are resume, GitHub, projects, and contact paths obvious?
- Are strongest projects easy to find?
- Are skills grouped clearly?
- Does the site avoid generic AI wording?

### Executive Scan

Ask:

- Does the page feel mature and trustworthy?
- Does it communicate risk reduction or operational value?
- Is the presentation calm and curated?
- Would a CEO or senior manager understand the point without reading every detail?
- Does the site feel like a professional proof system?

### Technical Credibility Scan

Ask:

- Are tools and workflows specific?
- Are claims supported?
- Is GitHub evidence visible?
- Are metrics accurate and not overused?
- Are cybersecurity visuals tied to real work?

### Visual Polish Scan

Ask:

- Is typography hierarchy clear?
- Are sections paced well?
- Are there too many same-weight cards?
- Is spacing intentional?
- Are colors and shadows consistent?
- Are important surfaces visually prioritized?

### Motion Restraint Scan

Ask:

- Does motion support attention?
- Is there too much animation?
- Is reduced-motion respected?
- Are hover/reveal patterns consistent?

### Duplicate Proof Scan

Ask:

- Is the same claim repeated nearby?
- Is the same metric shown in multiple adjacent surfaces?
- Are tool lists repeated without adding context?
- Could one stronger surface replace two weaker ones?

### Mobile Premium Scan

Ask:

- Does the page feel intentionally designed on mobile?
- Are CTAs visible and not cramped?
- Is there horizontal overflow?
- Are long technical strings wrapped safely?
- Is the first viewport useful?

---

## Acceptance Criteria

A Round 3 polish task is successful when:

- It improves clarity, trust, or premium feel
- It preserves supported claims
- It avoids unnecessary new dependencies
- It does not create duplicate proof
- It remains responsive
- It respects accessibility basics
- It has scoped validation appropriate to risk
- It updates tracking only as needed

---

## Stop Conditions

Stop and ask Bryan before:

- Rewriting substantive copy
- Changing supported metrics
- Removing major content
- Adding new dependencies
- Introducing new assets with unclear licensing
- Changing routes or navigation strategy
- Replacing a page's purpose
- Archiving or deleting project memory/history

---

## Final Question

Before finishing, answer:

```text
Did this make the site feel more premium and more credible, or did it just add more UI?
```

If the answer is "just more UI," revise or remove the change.
