# 01_EXECUTIVE_EXPERIENCE_ARCHITECT.md - Executive Experience Review

## Purpose

Use this architect when a task affects first impressions, homepage structure, section pacing, messaging hierarchy, trust signals, or the way a non-technical decision maker would experience the site.

This file complements recruiter scanning. Recruiter scanning asks: can someone quickly find Bryan's resume, skills, projects, and contact paths?

Executive experience asks: does the site feel mature, credible, calm, and worth trusting?

---

## Executive Review Questions

For every major section, ask:

- Can a non-technical executive understand the value in 5 seconds?
- Does this section communicate risk reduction, operational maturity, or trust?
- Does the content feel curated or dumped onto the page?
- Is the section too dense for a first read?
- Are metrics used selectively, or repeated until they lose impact?
- Does the visual treatment feel premium, or does it feel like a template/card grid?
- Does the section prove something real, or merely decorate the page?
- Does the page feel calm and confident, or busy and defensive?

---

## Executive-Friendly Signals

Emphasize:

- Risk reduction
- Operational proof
- Security maturity
- Clear technical documentation
- Tool fluency connected to outcomes
- Evidence of prioritization and judgment
- Real-world remediation and detection workflows
- GitHub evidence as proof, not decoration

Avoid leading with:

- Dense tool lists before value is clear
- Too many acronyms without context
- Overly technical strings in first-read areas
- Repeated dashboards
- Visual noise
- Claims that sound inflated or unsupported

---

## CEO Test

A strong section should answer at least one of these quickly:

```text
What problem did Bryan help solve?
How did he approach it?
What was the measurable or visible result?
Why should I trust the work?
```

If a section does not answer any of those, simplify, reposition, or remove it.

---

## Tone Guidance

Use calm, direct language.

Prefer:

- "Reduced risk through..."
- "Validated with..."
- "Documented evidence in..."
- "Prioritized remediation by..."
- "Mapped investigation steps to..."

Avoid:

- "Passionate"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Dynamic"
- "Robust"
- "World-class"
- "Next-generation"
- "Cyber ninja/operator/hacker" framing

---

## Section-Level Guidance

Hero:

- Must communicate who Bryan is, what security work he does, and where the proof lives.
- Avoid adding extra hero widgets unless they improve first-read clarity.
- Proof panel should feel like a refined evidence surface, not a novelty dashboard.

Metrics:

- Use selectively.
- Avoid presenting the same metric in multiple adjacent places.
- Tie every metric to a supported source.

Projects:

- Should read like operational case studies.
- Problem and outcome should be easy to scan.
- GitHub links should support credibility, not compete with the case study.

Contact:

- Should feel intentional, not like a sparse afterthought.
- Primary actions should be obvious without adding clutter.
